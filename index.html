ACTIVE:Adversarial Camouflage for Transferable and Intensive Vehicle Evasion

Anonymous

Abstract

In this paper, we introduce Adversarial Camouflage for Transferable and Intensive Vehicle Evasion (ACTIVE), a state-of-the-art physical camouflage attack framework that unleashes the full potential of its predecessor for 3D objects. Our version adds new features to the DTA, enhancing robustness and universality while overcoming its drawbacks of limited naturalness,as confirmed by our extensive experiments on 15 different models. In detail, robustness is improved by employing a new attack loss function to minimize the maximum score of all valid classes (instead of only one target class), and by establishing a Random Output Augmentation (ROA) module. Next, universality is enhanced by optimizing the texture over multiple 3D vehicle models. Furthermore, naturalness is improved by adding a modified smooth loss and bilinear interpolation to obtain a smooth-yet-realistic car texture similar to graffiti, as opposed to a colorful mosaic pattern that looks unnatural and suspicious. We also propose our camouflage loss to perform better in human vision and remove redundant features, for a more efficient architecture and process. Our evaluation shows that ACTIVE consistently outperforms other state-of-the-art frameworks on both single and two-stage publicly available detectors, including the latest YOLOv7. In fact, our universality evaluation even shows promising transferability to another task (i.e., the segmentation model) and to the real world, not just to other cars in different settings.

More Robust

We employ a new attack loss func-tion and incorporate a Random Output Augmentation(ROA) module by augmenting the rendered adversarial examples from the original DTA using various digitaltransformations to increase the robustness.



More Universal

We use multiple 3D vehiclemodels instead of one to increase universality. We collectmultiple vehicle datasets from Unreal Engine and train asingle DTN model to enable rendering for various vehicles. We verify that our ACTIVE can generate arobust universal attack pattern for various vehicles. This isadvantageous compared to the previous works that utilizelegacy texture mapping on neural rendering, which cannotgenerate a universal adversarial camouflage due to mappingbeing dependent on the 3D object.



More Natural

We incorporate a smooth loss anda camouflage loss to provide more naturalness. Wealso modify the texture projection function to gener-ate smoother texture compared to the original DTA.



More Efficient

We improve efficiency by reducing unnecessary factors, such as the number of DTN training datasets and model layers. First, we use ninecolors instead of 50 random colors to construct the DTNtraining datasets. We select eight boundary colors in theRGB color space (Red [255, 0, 0], Blue [0, 255, 0], Green[0, 0, 255], etc.) and one middle color (Gray [128, 128,128]). We expect that training the DTN to render nine col-ors representative of the RGB color space will allow it togeneralize to all other colors. Further, we remove the Trans-formation Feature (TF) Subtractor and TF Adder, two layerswithin the Expected Texture Transformer (a component in DTN) that we have identified as redundant, and replace thefinal activation function with sigmoid.
